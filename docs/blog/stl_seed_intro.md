# Soft verifiers without the noise floor: STL robustness as a process verifier for small LLMs

I built `stl-seed` to test a single conjecture from the SERA paper: that small open-weights language models, paired with a soft verifier and supervised fine-tuning on filtered process data, can reach competence on tasks they fail under standard sampling. SERA's evidence comes from a domain (visual reasoning, patch-overlap proxies) where the soft verifier is itself a learned, noisy approximation of the gold criterion. The interesting question is whether the recipe survives in a domain where the verifier is mathematically primitive. I picked Signal Temporal Logic (STL) robustness on biomolecular ODEs because in that setting the verifier reduces to recursive `min` and `max` over float64, and the noise term in the Goodhart decomposition collapses to round-off.

Day-1 result, shipped 2026-04-26: real `Qwen3-0.6B-bf16` plus standard sampling fails 4 of 4 hard biomolecular control specs (mean robustness in `{-247.6, -100.0, -0.5, -1.4}`). Beam-search warmstart, a structural sampler that enumerates a dense action lattice under a model-predictive constant-extrapolation lookahead, rescues all 4 (mean robustness in `{+25.0, +30.0, +0.002, +0.85}`, 12 of 12 seeds satisfy). The methodology gap is real, it is large, and it is not an artifact of a flat-prior strawman because the same Qwen3-0.6B prior is used across both samplers. This post explains what the artifact actually shows, what it does not show, and where I think the open question lands.

## Why STL?

Goodhart's law in process supervision can be written as a two-term decomposition:

```
R_gold - R_proxy = (R_gold - R_spec) + (R_spec - R_verifier).
```

The left side is the gap between what we care about (gold reward) and what we optimise (the proxy that the verifier emits). The first term on the right is the spec-completeness gap: how well the formal specification, no matter how perfectly evaluated, captures the human notion of "good." The second term is the verifier-fidelity gap: how well the chosen verifier estimates the spec it is meant to implement.

For a learned verifier, the second term is the noise floor: a process reward model trained on rollouts has bias from finite data and variance from the parameterisation, and these compose with the policy gradient in nontrivial ways. For formal STL evaluated by the recursive Donze and Maler 2010 semantics, the same term collapses to float64 round-off accumulated through the evaluator's `min` and `max` depth. In our specs that depth never exceeds 12, so the verifier-fidelity contribution is bounded above by 12 ulp of the leading scale, and is empirically below 1e-6 after the sigmoid squashing used downstream. This is a measurement bound, not a derived zero, and I report it that way in the README.

What this buys is an honest comparison axis. Ablate the verifier and the only term left is `(R_gold - R_spec)`. On glucose-insulin, the trajectory adversary in `src/stl_seed/analysis/adversary.py` finds a satisfying trajectory whose gold-scorer score is 2.27 robustness-units worse than a random spec-satisfying baseline. That number is an existence-style lower bound under hand-tuned blend weights for the gold scorer, not a population mean against an external oracle. I am explicit about this in the paper because the temptation to cite the gap as a clean number is real and the temptation to read it as a clean number is also real. It is neither.

## The result

The unified comparison harness sweeps 9 samplers across 5 task families with N=4 fixed seeds and 95 percent bootstrap confidence intervals. Under a flat-prior synthetic LLM the picture is clean: gradient-guided STL decoding lifts mean robustness on `glucose_insulin.tir.easy` from `+2.54` to `+20.00` (saturating the spec ceiling), and beam-search warmstart resolves the three narrow-vocabulary attractors (`bio_ode.{repressilator, toggle, mapk}`) where continuous methods fail by tens to hundreds of robustness units. I shipped this as Tier-3 of the falsification ladder and held the artifact there for 48 hours while I built the Tier-4 head-to-head: the same harness, same seeds, but with a real Qwen3-0.6B-bf16 prior wired into the sampler.

The Tier-4 numbers, in two sentences. With Qwen3-0.6B and standard sampling: `bio_ode.repressilator -247.6`, `bio_ode.toggle -100.0`, `bio_ode.mapk -0.5`, `cardiac.suppress_after_two -1.4`, all 0 of 3 seeds satisfying. With Qwen3-0.6B and beam-search warmstart: `+25.0`, `+30.0`, `+0.002`, `+0.85`, all 12 of 12 seeds satisfying.

Two honest caveats. First, the satisfying-corner vocabulary on repressilator and toggle is in scope by construction at `k_per_dim=5`, so the structural-search win is partly a statement about the lattice rather than the algorithm. I made the lattice transparent in the code and the paper rather than dressing it up. The right reading is that beam-search wins because the satisfying region of these specs is discrete and finite, and once you have any verifier signal at all, structural enumeration over the lattice is the cheapest move. Second, three of the four specs show zero variance across seeds, because Qwen3-0.6B at temperature 0.5 has logits sharp enough that the sampler is effectively deterministic. The rescue is real; the across-seed CI is degenerate. The fix is either a hotter sampling temperature or a larger model, and I will report both in the Phase 2 sweep. Both caveats belong in the artifact, and both are surfaced in the README before the headline number.

## What the methodology gap actually means

Beam-search warmstart is not a clever trick. It is the cheapest move available once you accept that the satisfying region of these specs is a discrete attractor in action space, not a smooth basin. Continuous gradient methods on smooth landscapes work because `grad rho` is locally informative about the direction toward higher robustness; on the bio_ode tasks the satisfying region is a single corner of the action box (repressilator `(0, 0, 1)`, toggle `(0, 1)`) or a non-trivial pulse pattern (MAPK), and `grad rho` away from that corner points back into a region where everything is roughly equally bad.

The literature is comfortable with this dichotomy in classifier-guided generation. DPS (Chung et al. 2023) and the broader classifier-guidance line work because the classifier's gradient is a continuous local signal; the failure modes documented in the controllable-text-generation literature are precisely the cases where the constraint is structural rather than continuous. STLCG and STLCG++ (Leung et al. 2020, 2022) introduce backpropagatable STL semantics for exactly the smooth-basin regime. The novelty in `stl-seed` is the empirical decomposition: a sampler bench that pre-registers which sampler is expected to win which task class, and reports both the wins and the losses on the same axes.

The methodology gap therefore does not say "Qwen3-0.6B is bad." It says that on the bio_ode tasks, a sub-billion-parameter prior plus standard sampling is information-poor about which corner of the action box satisfies the spec, and any sampler that does not exploit verifier feedback at decoding time will fail. That is a property of the sampler-task pair, not of the model. The honest framing for an SFT story is that filtered SFT should fold the structural-search information into the prior, so that at deployment time standard sampling becomes adequate. Whether that fold-in survives quantization and out-of-distribution specs is the question Phase 2 is meant to answer.

## The open question I'd send to Dettmers

SERA frames the soft-verifier story in terms of a saturation curve: at small filter strength the soft signal helps; at large filter strength the verifier-fidelity noise dominates and the curve bends back. The diagnosis is noise-scale: the soft signal carries finite information and finite bias, and the optimum filter strength is the bias-variance frontier. STL on formal ODE specs takes the noise-scale to round-off, which would predict that the curve never bends back, and within compute the SFT pilot in `src/stl_seed/training/` is consistent with that prediction (the curve flattens but does not invert).

The question I cannot answer from one repo is whether SERA's saturation is fundamentally a noise-scale story or fundamentally an information-geometry story. The two predict the same first-order behaviour at small filter strength but diverge at large filter strength: a noise-floor story bends back, an information-geometry story flattens. STL gives a clean experimental handle on the noise-floor axis while leaving the information-geometry axis untouched. If the soft-verifier conjecture is fundamentally about the geometry of the proxy manifold rather than the variance of the proxy estimate, the STL ablation should saturate without bending; if it is fundamentally about variance, the STL ablation should saturate at a higher ceiling than the patch-overlap ablation. That comparison fits in a single SFT factorial.

## What's next

Phase 2 is a $25 RunPod 4090-spot SFT sweep, 3 model sizes by 3 filter strengths by 2 task families, queued and ready to fire. The factorial is small but pre-registered, with a falsification-shaped readout: if filtered SFT does not transfer the structural-search advantage into the deployed sampler, I will say so. The pilot quantization-by-verifier study is running locally and is the first study where the verifier-fidelity term is small enough that quantization-induced noise becomes visible above it; if the curve there bends back at int4 but holds at int8, that is an interesting result on its own.

Beyond that, the coding-agent extension is the obvious next domain. Pytest-as-verifier and STL-on-runtime-traces both fit the same framework, and the methodology gap is plausibly larger there because the satisfying region of "code that passes the test suite" is a more discrete attractor than "ODE trajectory in time-in-range." A coding-agent variant of the same falsification ladder, with the same beam-search-warmstart structural sampler against standard autoregressive decoding under a small open-weights coder, is the cheapest experiment that would either confirm the same methodology gap in a second domain or fail to.
