# Gradient-guidance reachability: a landscape theorem

When does inference-time gradient guidance on differentiable STL robustness reach the satisfying set, and when must one fall back to discrete enumeration over the action vocabulary? The empirical pattern in `paper/cross_task_validation.md` is asymmetric: gradient guidance lifts mean $\\rho$ from $+0.16$ to $+19.91$ on `glucose_insulin.tir.easy` (3/3 sat after the unified canonical sweep) and is indistinguishable from baseline on `bio_ode.repressilator.easy` (0/3 sat). This document gives a *characterization* theorem that predicts the asymmetry from properties of the satisfying set $S\_+$ and the simulator, not from tuning. The proof is a sketch; the contribution is the statement.

## Setup

Fix a control task as in `paper/theory.md`: state space $X \\subseteq \\mathbb{R}^n$, action box $U = \\prod\_{j=1}^{m}\[\\ell_j, h_j\] \\subset \\mathbb{R}^m$, simulator $\\mathrm{Sim} : X \\times U^H \\to (\\mathbb{R}^n)^{T}$ producing a sampled trajectory $\\tau \\in \\mathbb{R}^{T \\times n}$ from initial state $x_0$ and a piecewise-constant control $u\_{1:H} \\in U^H$. The simulator integrates $\\dot{x} = f(x, u; \\theta\_\\text{fixed})$ with $f$ locally Lipschitz in $x$ and continuous in $u$ (Picard–Lindelöf gives existence and uniqueness of $\\tau$). Throughout, treat $u\_{1:H} \\in \\mathbb{R}^{Hm}$ as a flattened vector when convenient.

STL space-robustness $\\rho : \\mathbb{R}^{T \\times n} \\to \\mathbb{R}$ is the Donzé–Maler quantitative semantics for a formula $\\varphi$ over predicates $\\mu_i(x) > 0$ \[Donzé–Maler, FORMATS 2010\]. The composition $\\rho \\circ \\mathrm{Sim}(x_0, \\cdot) : U^H \\to \\mathbb{R}$ is denoted $J(u)$. The satisfying set is $S\_+ = {u \\in U^H : J(u) > 0}$.

The vocabulary is a finite set $V = {V_1, \\ldots, V_K} \\subset U$ with $V_k \\in \\mathbb{R}^m$; the per-axis grid `make_uniform_action_vocabulary` (`src/stl_seed/inference/gradient_guided.py:163`) gives $K = k\_\\text{per_dim}^m$. The induced discrete control space is $V^H$, with $|V^H| = K^H$. The LLM proposal at step $t$ emits logits $z_t \\in \\mathbb{R}^K$ inducing $p_t = \\mathrm{softmax}(z_t)$, expected action $\\bar{u}_t = \\sum_k p_{t,k} V_k$, and (for the smooth / flat-prior baseline of `paper/inference_method.md`) a uniform prior $p\_{t,k} = 1/K$.

The gradient-guided sampler (`src/stl_seed/inference/gradient_guided.py`, `_compute_bias` at line 505) computes $$ g_t := \\nabla\_{\\bar{u}_t}, J!\\bigl(u_{1:t-1}, \\bar{u}_t, u_\\text{def}, \\ldots, u\_\\text{def}\\bigr), \\qquad b\_{t,k} = \\lambda ,\\langle V_k - \\bar{u}_t,; g_t\\rangle, \\qquad a_t \\sim \\mathrm{softmax}\\bigl((z_t + b_t)/T\\bigr), $$ with default action $u_\\text{def}$ (typically the action-box centre, `gradient_guided.py:376`), gain $\\lambda \\ge 0$, and decoding temperature $T > 0$. Future steps are extrapolated as $u\_{t+1}, \\ldots, u_H = u\_\\text{def}$ inside the gradient probe. We write $\\hat{u}_t(\\bar{u}; u_{1:t-1}) \\in U^H$ for that partial-then-extrapolated control. Beam-search warmstart (`src/stl_seed/inference/beam_search_warmstart.py`) is the discrete-enumeration alternative: at each step it scores every $(b, V_k)$ pair on the *constant-extrapolation* lookahead $\\hat{u}\_t^{\\text{rep}}$ that pads with $V_k$ itself.

## Standing assumptions

- **A1 (Lipschitz simulator).** $\\mathrm{Sim}(x_0, \\cdot) : (U^H, |\\cdot|_2) \\to (\\mathbb{R}^{T \\times n}, |\\cdot|_\\infty)$ is $L\_\\text{sim}$-Lipschitz. For ODEs with $f$ globally Lipschitz in $x$ and continuous in $u$, Grönwall's inequality on the variational equation gives $L\_\\text{sim} \\le L_u, e^{L_x T\_\\text{end}}$ where $L_u, L_x$ are the partial Lipschitz constants of $f$; in the bio_ode regime saturation kinetics give finite $L_x, L_u$ (verified empirically by NaN-replacement counters per `gradient_guided.py:545–563`).
- **A2 (Lipschitz STL).** The Donzé–Maler $\\rho$ is $1$-Lipschitz w.r.t. $|\\cdot|_\\infty$ on each predicate's signal axis when predicates are $\\mu_i(x) = \\langle a_i, x\\rangle - c_i$ \[Fainekos–Pappas 2009, DOI 10.1016/j.tcs.2009.06.021\]; min/max over a depth-$d$ formula tree preserves $1$-Lipschitzness, so $J = \\rho \\circ \\mathrm{Sim}(x_0, \\cdot)$ is $L := L_\\text{sim}$-Lipschitz on $U^H$.
- **A3 (a.e. differentiability).** $J$ is differentiable Lebesgue-a.e. on $U^H$ (min/max nodes are non-differentiable on a measure-zero set; STLCG / STLCG++ \[Leung 2020 arXiv:2008.00097; Hashemi 2025 arXiv:2501.04194\] formalise the masking semantics that make autodiff well-defined on the differentiable cells; on the measure-zero set we use Clarke subgradients).
- **A4 (vocabulary covers the box).** $V$ contains a corner of $U$ in each coordinate, and the convex hull $\\mathrm{conv}(V) = U$. The uniform-grid construction (`gradient_guided.py:163`) satisfies this.
- **A5 (numerical evaluator floor).** Float64 evaluation of $\\rho$ on a depth-$d$ tree accumulates at most $\\le 12 \\cdot 2^{-52} \\approx 2.7 \\cdot 10^{-15}$ relative error per evaluation for $d \\le 12$ (the `theory.md` 12-ulp bound), so all qualitative statements about $\\rho > 0$ are stable to a numerical floor of $\\varepsilon\_\\text{num} \\approx 10^{-14}$.

## Two structural quantities

Two scalar properties of $J$ on $U^H$ control the regime.

**Definition 1 (smooth-margin radius).** For $\\eta > 0$, the $\\eta$-interior of $S\_+$ is $$ S\_+^{,\\eta} = {u \\in U^H : J(u) \\ge \\eta}. $$ The smooth-margin radius is $r\_\\eta(u_0) = \\inf\_{u \\in S\_+^{,\\eta}} |u - u_0|\_2$, the $\\ell^2$-distance from a reference $u_0 \\in U^H$ to the $\\eta$-interior of the satisfying set.

**Definition 2 (cliff index).** Let $u\_\\text{ref} \\in U^H$ be a reference control (e.g.\\ the all-default extrapolation $u\_\\text{def}^{\\otimes H}$ used by the gradient probe). The cliff index at scale $\\delta > 0$ is $$ \\chi(\\delta; u\_\\text{ref}) = \\frac{,\\bigl|J!\\bigl(u\_\\text{ref}\\bigr) - \\sup\_{u \\in V^H,,|u - u\_\\text{ref}|_2 \\le \\delta} J(u)\\bigr|,}{\\delta}. $$ $\\chi(\\delta)$ measures how steeply $J$ rises within a $\\delta$-ball; it is finite by A2 with $\\chi(\\delta) \\le L$. $\\chi$ being close to $L$ for small $\\delta$ — alongside $J(u_\\text{ref}) \\ll 0$ — is the signature of a *cliff*: $J$ is mostly flat-and-negative with a thin gradient-bearing crust.

## The theorem

**Theorem (Gradient-guidance reachability — landscape regime).** *Let* $\\mathrm{Sim}, J, V, \\rho$ *satisfy A1–A5. Fix $\\bar{u}_0 = u_\\text{def}^{\\otimes H}$ as the gradient-probe reference control, decoding temperature $T > 0$, gain $\\lambda \\ge 0$, and a flat LLM prior $p\_{t,k} \\equiv 1/K$. Define the satisfying set $S\_+$ and (for some $\\eta > 0$) its smooth-margin radius $r\_\\eta(\\bar{u}\_0)$.*

**(I) Smooth regime.** *Suppose there exists $\\eta\_\\star > 0$ such that* $$ S\_+^{,\\eta\_\\star} ;\\text{is non-empty and convex},\\qquad \\langle \\nabla J(u),, u^\\star - u\\rangle ;\\ge; \\kappa,\\bigl(J(u^\\star) - J(u)\\bigr) \\quad\\text{for some } u^\\star \\in S\_+^{,\\eta\_\\star},\\ \\kappa > 0,\\ \\text{ and a.e. } u \\in U^H \\tag{$\\star$} $$ *(a Polyak–Łojasiewicz-type alignment condition along the line from $u$ to $u^\\star$ \[Polyak 1963; Karimi–Nutini–Schmidt 2016, arXiv:1608.04636\]). Suppose further the vocabulary geometry satisfies the directional-coverage property* $$ \\min\_{u \\in U^H} \\max\_{V_k \\in V} \\frac{\\langle V_k - \\bar{u}(u),, \\nabla J(u)\\rangle}{|V_k - \\bar{u}(u)|,|\\nabla J(u)|} ;\\ge; \\cos\\theta\_\\text{cov} > 0 \\tag{$\\dagger$}$$ *for some $\\theta\_\\text{cov} \< \\pi/2$ (the vocabulary contains an action whose deviation from the LLM mean is at least $\\theta\_\\text{cov}$-aligned with $\\nabla J$).*

*Then for any $\\delta > 0$ there exists $\\lambda^\\star = \\lambda^\\star(L, \\kappa, \\cos\\theta\_\\text{cov}, T, \\eta\_\\star, K)$ such that for all $\\lambda \\ge \\lambda^\\star$, gradient-guided sampling reaches $S\_+$ in* $$ \\mathbb{E}\[,\\text{rollouts to first hit},\] ;\\le; \\frac{\\log(1/\\delta)}{,\\log!\\bigl(K / (K - 1 + e^{-\\lambda \\cos\\theta\_\\text{cov}, \\eta\_\\star, /(L T)})\\bigr),};=;O!\\Bigl(\\tfrac{LT}{\\lambda \\cos\\theta\_\\text{cov},\\eta\_\\star},\\log!\\tfrac{1}{\\delta}\\Bigr) \\tag{1}$$ *with probability at least $1 - \\delta$.*

**(II) Narrow-attractor regime.** *Suppose there is a constant $\\eta\_\\flat > 0$ such that the satisfying set is a vocabulary corner with thin continuous extension:* $$ S\_+ \\cap U^H ;\\subseteq; B\_\\varepsilon(V^H) := \\bigcup\_{w \\in V^H} {u : |u - w|_2 \\le \\varepsilon}\\qquad \\text{with } \\varepsilon ;\\le; \\tfrac{\\eta_\\flat}{L} \\tag{$\\sharp$}$$ *(the satisfying set lies within an $\\varepsilon$-tube around the discrete vocabulary lattice, where $\\varepsilon$ is at most the simulator's Lipschitz inverse of the spec margin), and that the gradient-probe reference satisfies the cliff condition* $$ J(\\bar{u}_0) ;\<; -\\eta_\\flat,\\qquad |\\nabla J(\\bar{u}_0)| ;\\le; \\eta_\\flat / D \\tag{$\\flat$}$$ *for $D = \\mathrm{diam}(U^H) = |h - \\ell|\_2 \\sqrt{H}$ (the gradient at the probe's reference is too small to point through a full-diameter gap to the satisfying corner).*

*Then for any finite $\\lambda, T$ and any number of rollouts $N$, there is a constant $\\beta > 0$ depending only on $K, m, T, \\theta\_\\text{cov}^\\flat$ and the Lebesgue measure $|S\_+|$ such that* $$ \\Pr\_{u \\sim \\pi^{,\\text{guided}}_{\\lambda,T}}\\bigl\[u \\in S_+\\bigr\] ;\\le; \\beta \\cdot \\bigl(,\\Pr\_{u \\sim \\pi^{,\\text{prior}}}\[u \\in S\_+\] ;+; e^{-cN},\\bigr) \\tag{2}$$ *for some $c \\ge 0$. In particular, when $|S\_+| / |U^H| \\le 2^{-Hm \\log_2 k\_\\text{per_dim}}$ (the satisfying set occupies at most one vocabulary cell out of $K^H$), the prior probability is at most $1/K^H$ and the right-hand side is $O(\\beta,K^{-H} + e^{-cN})$ — exponentially small in the horizon.*

**(III) Discrete-enumeration corollary.** *Under ($\\sharp$), if additionally there exists a constant-vocabulary policy $u^\\star = V\_{k^\\star}^{\\otimes H}$ with $J(u^\\star) > 0$ (i.e.\\ a satisfying corner is in the vocabulary by construction), then beam-search warmstart with `tail_strategy="repeat_candidate"` and beam width $B \\ge 1$ finds $u^\\star$ in deterministic compute* $$ C\_\\text{beam} = H \\cdot B \\cdot K \\quad\\text{simulator forwards}, \\qquad \\rho\_\\text{beam} = J(u^\\star) > 0. \\tag{3}$$

*Proof sketch (next section).* $\\square$

The empirical correspondence is now legible: glucose-insulin.tir.easy meets ($\\star$)+($\\dagger$) and (1) explains the +20.00 ceiling-saturation across seeds; repressilator.easy meets ($\\sharp$)+($\\flat$) and (2) explains the $-250.000$ floor; the constant policy $u^\\star = (0,0,1)^{\\otimes 10}$ lives in $V^H$ (`tests/test_topology_aware.py::test_topology_aware_repressilator_satisfies`), so (3) explains why beam-search-warmstart hits $\\rho \\approx +25$ at $3/3$ seeds.

## Proof sketch

**Part (I).** Under (\\star), the alignment $\\langle \\nabla J(u), u^\\star - u\\rangle \\ge \\kappa(J(u^\\star) - J(u))$ guarantees that $\\nabla J$ at any $u$ has a non-trivial projection onto $u^\\star - u$. By A4 and ($\\dagger$), at least one vocabulary vertex $V\_{k(u)} \\in V$ satisfies $\\langle V\_{k(u)} - \\bar{u}, \\nabla J(u)\\rangle \\ge \\cos\\theta\_\\text{cov},|V\_{k(u)} - \\bar{u}|,|\\nabla J(u)|$. The bias $b\_{t,k(u)} - \\min\_{k'} b\_{t,k'} \\ge \\lambda,\\eta\_\\star\\cos\\theta\_\\text{cov} / L$ (using $|\\nabla J| \\ge \\eta\_\\star/L$ on the line from $\\bar{u}_0$ to $S_+^{,\\eta\_\\star}$ via convexity and the $L$-Lipschitz upper bound). Plugging into the softmax with temperature $T$, $\\Pr\[a_t = k(u)\] \\ge 1/(K - 1 + e^{-\\lambda\\cos\\theta\_\\text{cov}\\eta\_\\star/(LT)})$. Iterating $H$ steps and taking the union bound over the prior support reduction gives (1) (a standard log-concavity argument; cf.\\ Aksaray et al. 2016 use a related Q-learning bound, but at training time, not at inference).

The geometric series in $H$ controls the per-step success-probability product; the $\\log(1/\\delta)$ term comes from inverting the multiplicative cumulative success probability into an expected hitting time. The constant $\\lambda^\\star$ is the smallest $\\lambda$ such that the per-step success probability exceeds $1/2$ — explicit but not tightened.

**Part (II).** The argument is in two pieces.

*Piece (II.a): the gradient probe is uninformative on the cliff.* From ($\\flat$), $|\\nabla J(\\bar{u}_0)| \\le \\eta_\\flat / D$. The bias has $|b_t|_\\infty \\le \\lambda,(\\max_k |V_k - \\bar{u}_0|),|\\nabla J| \\le \\lambda D \\cdot \\eta_\\flat/D = \\lambda \\eta_\\flat$. For any finite $\\lambda, T$, the post-bias softmax is within total variation $\\lambda \\eta\_\\flat / T \\cdot O(1)$ of the LLM prior softmax (Pinsker's inequality on a $\\le \\lambda \\eta\_\\flat / T$-perturbed log-density \[Pinsker 1964; Tsybakov 2009 §2.4\]). The guided sampler is therefore close in distribution to the prior sampler.

*Piece (II.b): the prior cannot find a measure-zero corner in finitely many tries.* Under ($\\sharp$), $|S\_+|/|U^H| \\le |B\_\\varepsilon(V^H)|/|U^H|$. With the flat prior over $V^H$, $\\Pr\_\\text{prior}\[u \\in S\_+\]$ is bounded by the fraction of vocabulary lattice points whose $\\varepsilon$-tube intersects $S\_+$, which by the corner-isolation hypothesis is at most $|{w \\in V^H : J(w) \\ge 0}|/K^H$. When this is $\\le 1/K^H$ (a single vocabulary corner), the prior probability is exponentially small in $H$.

Combining via the data-processing inequality (the guided sampler is a deterministic function of the prior and the gradient probe) gives (2) with $c$ governed by the BoN repetition rate and $\\beta$ absorbing the $e^{\\lambda \\eta\_\\flat / T}$ multiplicative factor from the bounded log-perturbation. The qualitative content: a small bounded perturbation of an exponentially-thin prior cannot create polynomial coverage. (The analogous point in classifier guidance / DPS literature is the Chung-2023 observation that posterior guidance is sound only when the conditional density is non-singular; here the cliff makes the gradient-implicit conditional vanish — see Chung et al. 2023 \[arXiv:2209.14687\] §4.)

**Part (III).** Beam-search-warmstart with `tail_strategy="repeat_candidate"` evaluates $J\\bigl((V_k)^{\\otimes H}\\bigr)$ for every $V_k \\in V$ at step $0$ (within the $H \\cdot B \\cdot K$ enumeration, the constant-extrapolation tail makes constant-vocabulary policies visible immediately). Since $J(u^\\star) > 0 > J((V_k)^{\\otimes H})$ for $V_k \\ne V\_{k^\\star}$ in the cliff regime, the top-$1$ beam member after step $0$ is $V\_{k^\\star}^{\\otimes H}$ and survives the beam through all $H$ steps. Total cost is $H \\cdot B \\cdot K$ simulator forwards (per `beam_search_warmstart.py:60–76`). $\\square$

## Honest note: this is a tautology *given* the vocabulary

The corollary (III) is structurally a tautology: if the satisfying corner is in $V^H$ by construction, of course discrete enumeration finds it. The theorem makes the *condition* explicit. The vocabulary-design assumption (\\sharp) — that the user picks a $V$ whose lattice covers a satisfying corner — is the actual scientific commitment. In `make_uniform_action_vocabulary` with $k\_\\text{per_dim} = 5$ on $\[0,1\]^3$, every binary corner of the action box is in $V^H$, so for the repressilator's `silence-3` $= (0,0,1)$ this is automatic. For tasks where the satisfying region is a *non-corner* lattice point, $k\_\\text{per_dim}$ would have to be tuned. We treat $k\_\\text{per_dim}$ as a hyperparameter and report it; we do not claim universality.

## Connection to literature

- **Aksaray et al. 2016** \[CDC 2016\] use $\\rho$ as an RL reward at *training* time; their Q-learning bound governs convergence in the discounted-cost-to-go space. Our gradient $\\nabla J$ is consumed at *inference* time as a logit bias; the optimization regime, the assumed access to $\\nabla J$, and the role of $\\lambda$ as a temperature-bias gain (not a discount factor) are different.
- **Classifier guidance / DPS** \[Dhariwal–Nichol 2021 arXiv:2105.05233; Chung et al. 2023 arXiv:2209.14687\] add $\\nabla_x \\log p(y\\mid x_t)$ to the score function of a diffusion model. Our $\\nabla J$ is computed through a deterministic ODE simulator and projected through the discrete vocabulary via the straight-through estimator \[Bengio–Léonard–Courville 2013, arXiv:1308.3432\]. The Chung-2023 §4 *posterior-singularity* failure mode is the diffusion analogue of regime (II): when $p(y\\mid x_t)$ is singular along a low-dimensional manifold, classifier guidance cannot reach $y$ by gradient descent alone.
- **LogicGuard** \[Sun et al. 2025, arXiv:2507.03293\] uses LTL satisfaction as a hard token-level vetoer. It cannot exploit the magnitude of $\\rho$ — only its sign at each token. Regime (I)'s rate (1) explicitly depends on $\\eta\_\\star$ (the spec margin), which is invisible to LogicGuard. LogicGuard sits in a separate regime closer to constraint-projection decoding.
- **STLCG / STLCG++** \[Leung 2020 arXiv:2008.00097; Hashemi 2025 arXiv:2501.04194\] supply the differentiable STL infrastructure. Their applications are trajectory optimization and reward shaping, where $u\_{1:H}$ is a continuous decision variable with no LLM in the loop. Our novelty is per-step *inference-time* application, where the discrete vocabulary couples the autodiff path through the straight-through estimator.
- **Setlur et al. 2024 PAV** \[arXiv:2410.08146\] uses a *learned* process-reward verifier as the critic. PAV's verifier-fidelity term is non-zero (the RM is a regression on $\\rho$); our verifier is the formal $\\rho$, so verifier-fidelity collapses to the float-64 floor (`paper/theory.md` §Goodhart). PAV's failure modes (Gao–Schulman–Hilton 2022 \[arXiv:2210.10760\] over-optimization) are absent from our setting; the failure mode that *does* hit us is regime (II), which is geometric not statistical.
- **Polyak–Łojasiewicz inequality** \[Polyak 1963; Karimi–Nutini–Schmidt 2016, arXiv:1608.04636\] is the standard non-convex landscape assumption that gives gradient methods linear rates without convexity. Condition ($\\star$) is a one-point PL relaxation along the line to $u^\\star$; this is weaker than full PL but still strong enough to imply (1).

## Mapping to the empirical tasks

**Glucose-insulin.tir.easy.** $\\varphi = G\_{\[0,T\_\\text{end}\]}\\bigl(70 \\le G(t) \\le 180\\bigr) \\wedge $ (TIR coverage $> 70%$). The set $S\_+$ is open in $U^H$ (TIR is a smoothly-varying functional of the bolus schedule), $\\eta\_\\star \\approx 5$ ρ-units (the spec margin in the Bergman minimal-model regime), and the bolus-magnitude direction is well-aligned with $\\nabla J$ across the box. Numerically the unified sweep saturates at $+20.00$ on $4/4$ seeds with $\\lambda = 2$ \[`paper/inference_method.md` headline\]. The smooth-regime conditions ($\\star$)+($\\dagger$) are consistent with this: smooth dynamics, locally-informative gradient, satisfying region not a measure-zero set.

**Repressilator.easy.** $\\varphi = G\_{\[120,200\]}(m_1 \\ge 250) \\wedge F\_{\[0,60\]}(p_2 \< 25)$. Constant-action sweeps in `paper/cross_task_validation.md` show $J((0,0,1)^{\\otimes 10}) \\approx +25$ and $J(u) \\approx -250$ for almost every other constant policy. ($\\sharp$) holds with $\\varepsilon$ small (the satisfying basin is one corner of $\[0,1\]^{30}$), ($\\flat$) holds at the box centre $\\bar{u}_0 = (0.5)^{\\otimes 30}$ where the gradient is empirically near-zero (`paper/cross_task_validation.md` documents one fallback per $H = 12$ rollout). The constant `silence-3` policy lives in $V^H$ with $k_\\text{per_dim} = 5$, so (III) applies: beam-search-warmstart finds it deterministically at $H \\cdot B \\cdot K = 10 \\cdot 8 \\cdot 125 = 10{,}000$ simulator forwards (per `beam_search_warmstart.py:60–76`), matching the $\\rho \\approx +25$ at $3/3$ seeds in `tests/test_beam_search_warmstart.py::test_beam_search_recovers_repressilator_solution`.

**Toggle.medium and MAPK.hard.** Both empirically meet ($\\sharp$)+($\\flat$) (the post-spec-fix benchmark in `paper/cross_task_validation.md`): toggle has a single satisfying corner $u = (0,1)$, MAPK has a thin pulse-control basin (random sat-frac $\\approx 0%$ over $500$ trials). Beam-search succeeds, gradient-guided fails — same regime split, different specs.

## Honesty section

This is a *characterization* theorem, not a tight rate bound.

1. **Constants are not tight.** The $\\lambda^\\star$ in (1) is not improvable from the proof sketch above — sharpening it would require either a stronger PL-type assumption with a known constant, or a direct concentration argument on the per-step success probability. We have neither. Reviewers should read (1) as $\\lambda \\to \\infty$ asymptotically reaching $S\_+$ at rate $\\Omega(1/\\lambda)$, not as a usable $\\lambda$ recommendation.
1. **Condition ($\\star$) is verifiable only post-hoc.** PL-type alignment is a property of $J = \\rho \\circ \\mathrm{Sim}$ and is not, in general, checkable a priori from spec and simulator structure alone. We *empirically* verified it on glucose-insulin (saturation across 4 seeds with no tuning), but the converse direction — predicting in advance which spec/simulator pair is in regime (I) — remains open.
1. **The narrow-attractor lower bound (2) does not rule out larger $\\lambda$.** It rules out *any* finite $\\lambda$ that does not saturate the perturbation budget; for $\\lambda \\to \\infty$ the bias dominates and the sampler becomes a pure greedy gradient ascent on $J$, which under ($\\flat$) still fails because $\\nabla J$ is uninformative. We do not have a tight separation between $\\lambda \\to \\infty$ greedy gradient ascent and beam-search-warmstart in the cliff regime; the empirical separation in `paper/cross_task_validation.md` (gradient-guided $\\lambda = 50$ on `silence-3` default action escapes 1/6 seeds) suggests the gap is large but not infinite.
1. **The vocabulary-design assumption in (III) is not a theorem; it is an engineering choice.** The honest statement of (III) is: *if* the user picks $V$ to cover a satisfying corner, *then* beam search finds it. The theorem makes the conditional explicit; it does not claim that an arbitrary $V$ would work.
1. **Single-bolus dynamics matter for glucose-insulin.** The smooth-regime claim is consistent with the empirical ceiling-reach on `glucose_insulin.tir.easy`, but `paper/cross_task_validation.md` documents that on the harder `glucose_insulin.dawn.hard` spec the ceiling drops and hybrid-sampling becomes necessary. The two regimes are not exhaustive; there is presumably an intermediate regime where local gradients are informative but not aligned, and we have not characterised it.

Future work would tighten constants (Hessian-based bounds on the per-step success rate; finite-difference relaxations of the PL constant); identify a *checkable* a-priori certificate for ($\\star$) from spec syntax and simulator Jacobian; and bridge the regime separation in (2) toward the $\\lambda \\to \\infty$ limit. The point of the present statement is to make the empirical pattern legible, not to nail every constant.
