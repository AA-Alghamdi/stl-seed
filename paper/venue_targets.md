# Venue targets. STL-seed Phase 1 artifact

Compiled 2026-04-26. Public repo: https://github.com/AA-Alghamdi/stl-seed. Artifact contribution: a falsifiable inference-time test of SERA's soft-verifier conjecture, using STL robustness as a *formal* soft signal on biomolecular ODE control with small open-weights LLMs (Qwen3-0.6B). 9-sampler comparison + 5 task families + a real-LLM falsification test. Apache 2.0. First publication for the author (Berkeley chem '26 → CMU MS AIE '26), with a senior advisor letter available.

Honest scope caveat: this is **Phase 1**, an inference-time-only result. No SFT sweep, no equivalence-at-scale claim. Venues expecting a full training-time story are excluded from the top tier.

## Recommended top 3 (in submit order)

### 1. ICML 2026. Failure Modes in Agentic AI (FMAI). primary target

**Why.** Topical fit is exact. The workshop's three pillars are (i) failure taxonomies and operational definitions, (ii) trace diagnostics and closed-loop evaluation, (iii) "training and systems interventions with verified trade-offs." STL-seed is *literally* a verified-trade-off study of inference-time interventions on a closed-loop biomolecular control task. STL robustness gives the verified part, the 9-sampler comparison gives the trade-off, and the real-LLM Qwen3-0.6B test gives the failure-mode diagnostic. Deadline 2026-05-08 AoE is ~12 days out, which is the right pressure for a focused 8-page write-up and aligns with the user's "submission within ~6 weeks" momentum target. Non-archival + dual-submission allowed means this does *not* burn the paper for L4DC 2027 or ICLR 2027. Acceptance probability for a well-written first submission with a senior letter is high (workshops are typically 40-60%, FMAI is new and likely under-subscribed at first edition). Strategic value: agentic-AI failure-modes is squarely in Dettmers' adjacent network (test-time compute, scaling laws, soft verification all overlap his Twitter/arXiv graph), and Seoul ICML draws that crowd.

### 2. ICML 2026. SCALE (Scalable & Verifiable Agentic AI). parallel target via late-breaking 3pp track

**Why.** Scope reads as if written for STL-seed: "test-time scaling laws for reasoning and planning," "safety, verification, and controllability under constraints," explicit "mixture-of-agents and expert routing" (which maps onto the 9-sampler comparison). The 3-page late-breaking track is undergrad-friendly and exactly the right size for the Phase 1 honest scope: it lets the user ship without overclaiming the SFT sweep. Deadline 2026-04-28 may already be at or past. if so, the FMAI 2026-05-08 deadline is the fallback and is more generous on length. Non-archival, so dual-submit with FMAI is ok unless an organizer says otherwise. Strategic value identical to FMAI: this is the test-time-compute crowd Dettmers reads.

### 3. NeurIPS 2026 Datasets & Benchmarks track. main archival venue

**Why.** This is the one main-conference track where the artifact's actual contribution shape. a 5-task-family + 9-sampler benchmark protocol with negative results, plus a falsifiable methodology. is explicitly invited rather than tolerated. The 2026 D&B CFP says "negative results and critical analyses" and "submissions need not introduce a new model or outperform prior work." Deadline 2026-05-06 is 10 days out. With a senior advisor letter and a clean writeup, D&B acceptance rate (~30%) is reachable for a careful first submission. Risk: the abstract+full window is tight (May 4 → May 6); the user should commit the abstract by Apr 30 to leave the buffer. Strategic value: a NeurIPS acceptance is the single most valuable artifact for the CMU MSAIE Aug 2026 cohort entry and for the Dettmers cold-outreach gating in the April 2026 CMU outreach campaign memory note.
