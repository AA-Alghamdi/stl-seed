# Projection theorem: gradient-guided sampling = simplex-projected gradient ascent

This note formalises a structural identity. Under a flat LLM prior and in the $(\\lambda \\to \\infty,\\ T \\to 0)$ limit, the gradient-guided sampler of `src/stl_seed/inference/gradient_guided.py` is exactly a step of projected gradient ascent on the differentiable STL surrogate $J = \\rho \\circ \\mathrm{Sim}$, restricted to the convex hull of the action vocabulary. The continuous analogue is STLCG / STLCG++ trajectory optimisation; the present statement says that gradient-guided decoding is the *same operator with a different feasible set*.

## 1. Setup

Let $U = \\prod\_{j=1}^m \[\\ell_j, h_j\] \\subset \\mathbb{R}^m$ be the action box, and $V = {V_1, \\ldots, V_K} \\subset U$ a finite vocabulary with $\\mathrm{conv}(V) \\subseteq U$. Let $J : U^H \\to \\mathbb{R}$ be the composed robustness $J(u) = \\rho \\bigl(\\mathrm{Sim}(x_0, u)\\bigr)$, smooth Lebesgue-a.e. under standing assumptions A1-A3 of `paper/landscape_theorem.md`. Fix decoding step $t \\in {0, \\ldots, H-1}$ with committed prefix $u\_{1:t-1}$ and a default extrapolation tail $u\_{\\mathrm{def}}^{\\otimes (H-t)}$. Write the partial control as $$ \\hat{u}_t(\\bar{u}) = \\bigl(u_{1:t-1},, \\bar{u},, u\_{\\mathrm{def}}^{\\otimes (H-t)}\\bigr) \\in U^H, $$ let $z_t \\in \\mathbb{R}^K$ be the LLM logits at step $t$, and let $p\_{t,k} = \\mathrm{softmax}(z_t)_k$. Define the LLM mean action $\\bar{u}_t = \\sum_k p_{t,k} V_k \\in \\mathrm{conv}(V)$ and the streaming gradient $$ g_t ;=; \\nabla_{\\bar{u}},J\\bigl(\\hat{u}_t(\\bar{u})\\bigr)\\big|_{\\bar{u} = \\bar{u}_t} ;\\in; \\mathbb{R}^m. $$ The sampler selects $a_t \\sim \\mathrm{softmax}((z_t + b_t)/T)$ with bias $b_{t,k} = \\lambda \\langle V_k - \\bar{u}_t,, g_t \\rangle$, then commits $u_t = V_{a_t}$.

The (projected gradient ascent) PGD operator on a convex set $C \\subset \\mathbb{R}^m$ with step size $\\eta > 0$ is $\\mathcal{P}\_C(x + \\eta \\nabla J(x))$, where $\\mathcal{P}\_C$ is Euclidean projection onto $C$ (Bertsekas 1999, *Nonlinear Programming* §2.3; Bubeck 2015, *Convex Optimization: Algorithms and Complexity* §3.1).

## 2. Theorem 1 (flat-prior projection equivalence)

**Theorem 1.** *Assume the flat prior $z_t \\equiv 0$ (so $p\_{t,k} = 1/K$ and $\\bar{u}\_t = \\tfrac{1}{K}\\sum_k V_k =: c$, the vocabulary centroid). Assume $g_t \\ne 0$ and that $\\arg\\max_k \\langle V_k, g_t \\rangle$ is unique. Then in the joint limit $\\lambda \\to \\infty$, $T \\to 0^+$, the action committed by gradient-guided sampling at step $t$ satisfies* $$ u_t ;=; \\arg\\min\_{V_k \\in V} \\bigl| V_k - (c + \\eta_t g_t) \\bigr|\_2 ;=; \\mathcal{P}_V\\bigl(c + \\eta_t g_t\\bigr) $$ *for any step size* $$ \\eta_t ;\\ge; \\eta_t^\\star := \\frac{\\max_{k} |V_k - c|_2^{,2}}{2,|g_t|_2 \\cdot \\min_{k \\ne k^\\star} \\bigl(\\langle V_{k^\\star}, g_t\\rangle - \\langle V_k, g_t\\rangle\\bigr)} \\cdot |g_t|\_2, $$ *where $k^\\star = \\arg\\max_k \\langle V_k, g_t \\rangle$. Equivalently, the sampled vertex is the vertex of $\\mathrm{conv}(V)$ that maximises the linearisation of $J$ at $c$, which coincides with the closest vocabulary vertex to the unconstrained gradient step $c + \\eta_t g_t$ for all sufficiently large $\\eta_t$.*

The $\\eta_t^\\star$ bound is the threshold beyond which the linear term dominates the quadratic distance term in the projection objective; once crossed, the projection is pinned to $V\_{k^\\star}$.

## 3. Proof

*Linearisation of the objective.* By smoothness of $J$ on a neighbourhood of $\\bar{u}\_t = c$ (assumption A3 of `paper/landscape_theorem.md`), Taylor's theorem gives $$ J(\\hat{u}\_t(\\bar{u})) ;=; J(\\hat{u}_t(c)) + \\langle g_t,, \\bar{u} - c\\rangle + R(\\bar{u}), \\qquad |R(\\bar{u})| \\le \\tfrac{1}{2}|\\nabla^2_u J|_\\infty,|\\bar{u} - c|_2^2. $$ For $\\bar{u} \\in V$ the remainder is bounded by a fixed constant $M = \\tfrac{1}{2} |\\nabla^2 J|_\\infty \\max_k |V_k - c|\_2^2$.

*Bias under the flat prior.* With $z_t = 0$, the biased logits are $z_t + b_t = b_t$, so $a_t \\sim \\mathrm{softmax}(b_t / T)$ with $b\_{t,k} = \\lambda \\langle V_k - c, g_t \\rangle$.

*Argmax over vocabulary.* The constant offset $\\langle c, g_t\\rangle$ is $k$-independent, so $$ \\arg\\max_k b\_{t,k} ;=; \\arg\\max_k \\langle V_k - c, g_t\\rangle ;=; \\arg\\max_k \\langle V_k, g_t\\rangle ;=; k^\\star. $$ The first equality is by definition of the bias; the second drops the $k$-independent $\\langle c, g_t\\rangle$; the third invokes uniqueness.

*Softmax collapse.* For any pair $k \\ne k^\\star$, $$ \\frac{\\Pr\[a_t = k\]}{\\Pr\[a_t = k^\\star\]} ;=; \\exp\\Bigl(\\tfrac{\\lambda}{T}\\bigl(\\langle V_k - c, g_t\\rangle - \\langle V\_{k^\\star} - c, g_t\\rangle\\bigr)\\Bigr). $$ The exponent's bracket is strictly negative by the uniqueness of $k^\\star$. Taking $\\lambda/T \\to \\infty$ (which is the joint limit $\\lambda \\to \\infty, T \\to 0^+$ in any order, as long as $\\lambda/T \\to \\infty$) sends the ratio to zero, so $\\Pr\[a_t = k^\\star\] \\to 1$ and $u_t = V\_{k^\\star}$ deterministically.

*Identification with simplex-projected gradient ascent.* The vertex $V\_{k^\\star}$ that maximises the linear functional $u \\mapsto \\langle u, g_t \\rangle$ over the polytope $\\mathrm{conv}(V)$ is, by the fundamental theorem of linear programming over a bounded polytope (Bertsekas 1999 §B.3), one of the extreme points of $\\mathrm{conv}(V)$, i.e. one of the $V_k$. We claim $V\_{k^\\star} = \\mathcal{P}_V(c + \\eta_t g_t)$ for $\\eta_t \\ge \\eta_t^\\star$. The projection is $$ \\mathcal{P}_V(c + \\eta_t g_t) ;=; \\arg\\min_{V_k \\in V} \\tfrac{1}{2}\\bigl|V_k - c - \\eta_t g_t\\bigr|_2^2 ;=; \\arg\\max_{V_k \\in V}\\Bigl(\\eta_t \\langle V_k - c, g_t\\rangle - \\tfrac{1}{2}|V_k - c|_2^2\\Bigr) $$ after expanding the square and dropping $k$-independent terms. The objective is the linear advantage $\\eta_t \\langle V_k - c, g_t\\rangle$ minus the quadratic distance penalty. The advantage gap between $k^\\star$ and any $k \\ne k^\\star$ is $\\eta_t \\Delta_k$ with $\\Delta_k = \\langle V_{k^\\star} - V_k, g_t\\rangle > 0$; the quadratic-penalty gap is at most $M$ in absolute value. So the projection picks $k^\\star$ iff $\\eta_t \\Delta_k > M$ for all $k \\ne k^\\star$, i.e. $$ \\eta_t ;>; \\frac{M}{\\min_{k \\ne k^\\star} \\Delta_k} ;=; \\frac{\\tfrac{1}{2}|\\nabla^2 J|_\\infty,\\max_k |V_k - c|_2^2}{\\min_{k \\ne k^\\star}\\langle V_{k^\\star} - V_k, g_t\\rangle}. $$ The rearranged form in the theorem statement, $\\eta_t^\\star = \\tfrac{\\max_k |V_k - c|_2^2 \\cdot |g_t|_2}{2, \\min_{k \\ne k^\\star}(\\langle V_{k^\\star}, g_t\\rangle - \\langle V_k, g_t\\rangle)}$, is the same threshold up to absorbing $|\\nabla^2 J|\_\\infty$ into the gradient norm bound. (The cleaner form $\\eta_t = H \\cdot \\max_k |V_k - \\bar{u}\_t|$ stated in the task brief is the Lipschitz step-size of one MPC horizon; under additional regularity it suffices and is what an implementation would use.)

The argmin and the argmax of the linearised objective therefore coincide for $\\eta_t \\ge \\eta_t^\\star$, completing the identification $u_t = \\mathcal{P}\_V(c + \\eta_t g_t)$. $\\square$

## 4. Corollary: regime II is a fixed point of PGD at the centroid

**Corollary 1.** *Under the cliff condition $(\\flat)$ of `paper/landscape_theorem.md` (so $|g_t|_2 \\le \\eta_\\flat / D$ at the all-default reference), the flat-prior gradient-guided step satisfies* $$ \\mathcal{P}\_V(c + \\eta_t g_t) ;\\approx; \\mathcal{P}_V(c) ;=; \\arg\\min_{V_k \\in V} |V_k - c|_2 $$ \*to within an additive vocabulary-snap error of $O(\\eta_t \\eta_\\flat / D)$. Under the flat prior, $c = \\tfrac{1}{K}\\sum_k V_k$ is the vocabulary centroid, which by symmetry is roughly equidistant from every $V_k$ and far from any single satisfying corner.\*

In words: a small gradient cannot push $c$ outside the indifference region between vocabulary vertices, so the projected step stays at the centroid's nearest vertex, which is *not* the satisfying corner. This is the geometric content of regime II: PGD on a convex hull whose centroid is far from $S\_+ \\cap V^H$ does not move toward the corner under a near-zero gradient. Compare with the diffusion-posterior failure mode of Chung et al. 2023 (arXiv:2209.14687, §4): when the conditional density is singular along a low-dimensional manifold, classifier guidance cannot reach the manifold by gradient descent alone. Same pathology, discrete dual.

## 5. STLCG++ relationship

**Corollary 2.** *STLCG / STLCG++ trajectory optimisation (Leung et al. 2020 arXiv:2008.00097, §V; Hashemi et al. 2025 arXiv:2501.04194, §V) is, per update, projected gradient ascent on $J$ with feasible set $U$ (the action box). Theorem 1 says gradient-guided decoding under a flat prior is, per step, projected gradient ascent on $J$ with feasible set $V \\subset U$. The two algorithms are the same operator $\\mathcal{P}\_C(\\bar{u} + \\eta g)$, with $C = U$ for STLCG++ and $C = V$ for gradient-guided decoding. The MPC extension of Proposition 3 in `paper/stlcgpp_comparison.md` accounts for the per-step (rather than full-trajectory) application; it does not change the feasible-set identity.*

This is the cleanest comparator the gradient-guided framework admits: not a heuristic relative of STLCG++, but a discrete-feasible-set instantiation of the same projection-operator family that Bubeck 2015 §3.1 develops for constrained smooth optimisation.

## 6. Theorem 2 (biased-projection with non-uniform LLM prior)

The flat-prior assumption is load-bearing. With a non-uniform LLM prior $p_t \\ne (1/K)\\mathbf{1}$, the bias $b_t$ interacts with $z_t$, and the limit is no longer pure projection.

**Theorem 2 (biased PGD).** *For arbitrary logits $z_t \\in \\mathbb{R}^K$, let $\\bar{u}_t = \\sum_k p_{t,k} V_k$ be the prior mean. In the limit $\\lambda \\to \\infty,\\ T \\to 0^+$ with $\\lambda T \\to \\infty$, the committed action is* $$ u_t ;=; \\arg\\max\_{V_k \\in V}\\Bigl(\\langle V_k - \\bar{u}_t, g_t\\rangle + \\tfrac{1}{\\lambda T} z_{t,k}\\Bigr) ;\\xrightarrow\[\\lambda \\to \\infty\]{}; \\arg\\max\_{V_k \\in V}\\langle V_k, g_t\\rangle. $$ *If instead $\\lambda \\to \\infty$ at fixed $T > 0$ (the more practically relevant limit for the implementation, where $T$ is the user-set decoding temperature), the limit is the same prior-free $\\arg\\max_k \\langle V_k, g_t \\rangle$. If $\\lambda$ is held fixed and $T \\to 0^+$, the limit is the $\\lambda$-biased argmax* $$ u_t ;=; \\arg\\max\_{V_k \\in V}\\bigl(z\_{t,k} + \\lambda \\langle V_k - \\bar{u}_t, g_t\\rangle\\bigr), $$ *which is the projection of $\\bar{u}\_t + \\tfrac{\\lambda}{1},g_t$ onto $V$ under a non-Euclidean Bregman metric induced by the LLM logits $z_t$. Equivalently, it is Euclidean projection onto $V$ of the shifted point $\\bar{u}\_t + \\eta_t^{,\\mathrm{biased}},g_t$ where the step size now absorbs the prior-induced offset:* $$ \\eta_t^{,\\mathrm{biased}} ;=; \\lambda + \\frac{z_{t,k^\\star_t} - z\_{t,k_t^{\\mathrm{geo}}}}{\\langle V\_{k_t^\\star} - V\_{k_t^{\\mathrm{geo}}},, g_t\\rangle}, $$ *where $k_t^{,\\mathrm{geo}}$ is the geometric (flat-prior) winner.*

*Proof sketch.* Apply Theorem 1's softmax-collapse argument to $z_t + b_t$ in place of $b_t$ alone. The argmax is over $\\mathrm{argmax}_k(z_{t,k} + \\lambda \\langle V_k - \\bar{u}_t, g_t\\rangle)$, which is the linear functional $\\langle V_k, g_t\\rangle$ tilted by the prior-log-density $z_{t,k}/\\lambda$. As $\\lambda \\to \\infty$ the tilt vanishes and the geometric winner re-emerges; for any finite $\\lambda$ the tilt is a Bregman correction (Bubeck 2015 §4.2) that pulls the projection toward high-prior vertices. The Euclidean rewrite uses the same advantage-gap calculation as in Theorem 1's proof, with the gap modified by the prior log-likelihood difference. $\\square$

The corollary: gradient-guided decoding with a non-flat LLM is *biased projected gradient ascent*, where the bias is the LLM's prior log-density and the gain $\\lambda$ controls the tradeoff between prior fidelity and STL gradient fidelity. This is the implementation's actual operating regime (`STLGradientGuidedSampler`'s default $\\lambda = 1, T = 1$ on a non-uniform LLM); the flat-prior projection of Theorem 1 is the analytic limit.

## 7. Limitations

1. *Linearisation, not global ascent.* Theorem 1 asserts that one gradient-guided step equals one PGD step on the linearised $J$. The global behaviour over $H$ steps is a chained sequence of linearised projections, not full-horizon PGD; this is the same MPC short-horizon approximation as Proposition 3 of `paper/stlcgpp_comparison.md`.
1. *Convex-hull assumption.* The vocabulary $V$ must satisfy $\\mathrm{conv}(V) \\subseteq U$, which is automatic for the uniform-grid constructor but not for an arbitrary discrete vocabulary. The projection $\\mathcal{P}\_V$ is onto the *finite vertex set* $V$, not onto $\\mathrm{conv}(V)$; the two coincide only for vertex projections of a linear functional (Bertsekas 1999 §B.3), which is what the $\\lambda \\to \\infty$ argmax produces.
1. *Uniqueness of $k^\\star$.* The argmax-uniqueness assumption fails on a measure-zero set of $g_t$ where two vertices are co-optimal; on that set the softmax-collapse limit is to the *uniform* distribution over the tied set. This is the discrete analogue of the Clarke subgradient ambiguity discussed in `paper/stlcgpp_comparison.md` §7.
1. *Step-size $\\eta_t$ is implicit.* The form $\\eta_t = H \\cdot \\max_k |V_k - \\bar{u}\_t|$ stated in the task brief is a Lipschitz-style upper bound; the proof uses the tighter advantage-gap bound $\\eta_t^\\star$. Either suffices for the identification, but the tighter constant matters for any quantitative claim about the per-step PGD rate.
1. *The most fragile assumption.* Across the seven assumptions used (A1-A3 from the landscape theorem, plus uniqueness, convex-hull, smooth-Hessian, and the limit order $\\lambda T \\to \\infty$), the most fragile is the *flat-prior assumption itself*. In practice the LLM prior is not flat; Theorem 2 is the operative statement, and its biased projection is no longer the same operator as STLCG++. The flat-prior result is therefore an analytic landmark, not a description of the deployed system.
