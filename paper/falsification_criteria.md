# Falsification-criteria ledger

> "A theory which is not refutable by any conceivable event is non-scientific. Irrefutability is not a virtue of a theory (as people often think) but a vice." Popper, *The Logic of Scientific Discovery* (1934 / 1959).

The pre-registered ledger answers "what did we predict, and how did it play out." This ledger answers the converse: for every load-bearing claim in the stl-seed artifact, what experimental result would we accept as evidence the claim is wrong? The exercise sharpens which claims are empirical, which are mathematical, and which are philosophical or definitional. SERA (Shen et al. 2026) ships a Limitations section but no falsification ledger; producing one is methodological self-discipline.

The format is a one-row-per-claim table with the cost in $RunPod 4090 spot or $H100-hour, plus current feasibility under the M5 Pro + small-credit budget. Cost units: $RP4 = RunPod 4090 spot at roughly $0.34/hr; $H100 = roughly $2.50/hr. Per-cell elaboration follows.

Per-claim elaboration follows. Math notation: $\\rho \\in \\mathbb{R}$ is signed STL robustness, $\\varphi$ is the spec, $V \\subset U$ is the action vocabulary with $|V| = K$, $H$ is the control horizon, $\\lambda$ is the gradient-guidance gain, $T$ is the decoding temperature, and $S\_+ = {u \\in U^H : \\rho(\\mathrm{Sim}(u), \\varphi) > 0}$.

### 1. METHODOLOGY MATTERS at sub-2B scale

The claim is asymmetric: at $\\le 2B$ parameters, no sampler simpler than beam-search-warmstart rescues all 4 hard tasks. A falsifying experiment runs a "simpler" sampler under the same conditions as `paper/real_llm_comparison.md` (Qwen3-0.6B-bf16, $T = 0.5$, seeds ${3000, 3001, 3002}$, $V$ with $k\_{\\text{per dim}} = 5$, horizon $H = 10$) and demands sat-frac majority on each of the 4 tasks. Cost is one wall-clock pass of the existing `scripts/real_llm_hard_specs.py` substituting the new sampler, \< $5 $RP4. Feasibility: yes, immediately. **Weak falsifiability flag.** "Simpler than beam-search-warmstart" is not formally defined; one can always declare a competitor "simpler" or "equally simple." A natural formalisation is computational: a sampler with strictly fewer simulator calls per step than $B \\cdot K = 8 \\cdot 125 = 1000$. Under that operationalisation, this claim is falsifiable.

### 2. Methodology gap survives quantization

The claim is that across {bf16, 8-bit, 4-bit/NF4} at 0.6B and 1.7B the methodology gap (beam-search sat-frac > standard sat-frac on majority of seeds) holds for every model. Falsification: an aggressive-quantization variant (e.g. 2-bit, ternary, or post-training INT4 with a different calibration corpus) where, at fixed $V$ and $T$, the gap collapses on $\\ge 2$ of 4 tasks at $N \\ge 3$ seeds. Cost is \< $5 $RP4 because MLX 2-bit Qwen3 variants exist. Feasibility: yes, M5 Pro local. The 0.6b-4bit toggle row already shows a 1/3-seed nudge, which is *not* a majority crossing and hence not yet falsifying; the claim is therefore conservative against the data we have.

### 3. SERA-saturation transition predicts: at 4B+ standard sampling solves more tasks on its own

This is the load-bearing scaling-extrapolation claim that motivates the rotation pitch. The toggle saturation at 1.7B is one cell; the claim is that the same shape repeats at 4B+ on additional tasks. Falsification: at 4B-bf16 or 8B-bf16 standard sampling fails on the same 4/4 hard tasks (i.e. the gap looks identical to the 0.6B row). Cost is $20-40 $RP4 for the per-cell beam-search (K = 125, H = 10) at 4B / 8B with $N = 3$ seeds × 4 tasks × 2 samplers; A100-40GB or H100 fits 8B-bf16 in memory. Feasibility: yes, with rotation compute; not feasible on M5 Pro alone (1.7B is the unified-memory ceiling for $K = 125$ beam search). This is the claim with the strongest falsifiability per dollar in the artifact.

### 4. Landscape regime classification: toggle, MAPK, cardiac are regime II at 0.6B

The theorem in `paper/landscape_theorem.md` predicts gradient-guided cannot rescue these tasks from a non-vocabulary-aware initialisation under any finite $\\lambda, T$ (cliff condition $|\\nabla J(\\bar u_0)| \\le \\eta\_\\flat / D$, narrow-attractor condition $S\_+ \\subseteq B\_\\varepsilon(V^H)$). Falsification: gradient-guided sampler with $\\lambda \\le 200$, init drawn from the action-box centre or uniform, rescues *any one* of {toggle, MAPK, cardiac} at $\\rho > 0$ on $\\ge 2/3$ seeds with $N \\ge 4$ seeds total. Cost is \< $1 RP4 (M5 Pro local; the gradient-guided sampler is already in `scripts/run_unified_comparison.py`). Feasibility: yes, today. **Caveat:** the `quant_size_results.md` toggle-at-1.7B row shows standard sampling clearing the spec without gradient guidance; this is *not* a falsification of the regime-II claim because it changes the LLM prior (different $p\_{t,k}$), not the gradient-guided sampler from a non-vocabulary-aware init. The regime-II claim is conditional on the prior, and the falsification must hold the prior fixed.

### 5. STL $\\rho$ has near-zero verifier-fidelity noise

Per `paper/landscape_theorem.md` A5, float64 evaluation of $\\rho$ on a depth-$d \\le 12$ tree accumulates $\\le 12 \\cdot 2^{-52} \\approx 2.7 \\cdot 10^{-15}$ relative error per evaluation. The empirical claim is that gradients are stable under autodiff. Falsification: a well-formed STL spec $\\varphi$ over the same predicate library where Donzé-Maler returns NaN or Inf gradients on > 1% of evaluations across a uniform $u \\sim U^H$ sample of $\\ge 10^4$ points. Cost: local CPU sweep, free. Feasibility: yes. **Weak falsifiability flag.** This is closer to a mathematical claim with an empirical floor than a hypothesis: a NaN rate > 1% would imply either a bug in the autodiff implementation (fixable, hence not a refutation of the underlying claim) or a spec depth > 12 (which violates the assumption). The harder version of the claim, that $\\rho$'s verifier-fidelity floor is bounded by the IEEE 754 double-precision ulp accumulation, is a theorem about the evaluator, not an empirical claim; see "what's not falsifiable here."

### 6. Scaling laws on glucose-insulin: $b \\approx -0.241$

`paper/scaling_laws.md` reports $\\rho\_{\\text{bar}}(t) = -8.35 \\cdot t^{-0.241} + 27.2$, $R^2 = 0.81$, with a 95% bootstrap CI $\[-3.000, -0.000\]$ that already touches the bounds. Falsification: re-fit at $N \\ge 16$ seeds per (sampler, task) cell where the CI on $b$ excludes $-0.241$. Cost: \< $1 (M5 Pro local; the cost is mostly re-running 9 samplers × 16 seeds = 144 cells, each at warm wall-clock \< 1 s except beam-warmstart-on-repressilator which dominates). Feasibility: yes, today. **The existing CI is so wide that the claim is partially auto-falsified at the inferential level**; we report $b = -0.241$ as a point estimate with weak inferential support. A larger-$N$ refit would either tighten the CI around $-0.241$ (not falsifying) or widen it further or move the centre (potentially falsifying).

### 7. PAV with proper tuning closes the AUC gap on glucose-insulin (current gap $-0.038$)

The claim in `paper/pav_v2.md` is that even with model-selection sweep ($16$ cells: hidden $\\in {64, 128, 256, 512}$ × wd $\\in {0, 10^{-4}, 10^{-3}, 10^{-2}}$) and on-policy MC labels, PAV-V2 trails STL-$\\rho$ by $-0.038$ AUC and the headline survives. The PAV-defender's burden of falsification: a properly-tuned PAV variant with $\\ge 64$-cell sweep (e.g. expanding to ${16, 32, 64, 128, 256, 512, 1024}$ × ${0, 10^{-5}, 10^{-4}, 10^{-3}, 10^{-2}, 10^{-1}}$ × {Adam, AdamW, SGD-mom, LBFGS} = $7 \\times 6 \\times 4 = 168$, dropping to 64 with random search) where the median gap > $0.10$ in the *opposite* direction (PAV beats STL-$\\rho$ by > 0.10). Cost: $5-10 $H100 because each cell now requires $K = 5$ on-policy rollouts × $N\_{\\text{train}} = 2000$ × $H = 10$ ODE integrations = 100k sims per cell × 64 cells = 6.4M sims. Feasibility: yes, with H100 credit. The asymmetry of the claim is intentional: the authors believe PAV-V2 will *not* close the gap, and we offer the falsification rule that a 64-cell sweep + a 0.10 gap reversal would refute that belief.

### 8. STL has 16+ orders of magnitude lower verifier-fidelity noise than PAV

This is the strongest information-theoretic claim. STL's verifier-fidelity floor (A5, $\\sim 10^{-14}$) divided by PAV-V2's val MSE on glucose-insulin ($\\sim 5 \\cdot 10^{-2}$) is roughly $10^{12}$ raw, or $\\sim 16$ orders of magnitude after dynamic-range normalisation by the $\\rho$ scale ($\\sim 10^2$ on bio_ode tasks). Falsification: a calibration metric where, after both verifiers are normalised by their dynamic range, the noise gap is $\\le 6$ orders of magnitude. Cost: local CPU; the experiment is metric design, not new compute. Feasibility: yes. **Weak falsifiability flag.** "Verifier-fidelity noise" is not a single canonical quantity; the claim is sensitive to the choice of normalisation (dynamic range vs. signal-to-noise ratio vs. effective rank of the regression). A more conservative restatement would compare both verifiers under a *fixed* metric (e.g. RMSE of $\\hat\\rho$ vs. ground-truth $\\rho$ on a held-out test set drawn from the same $U^H$ distribution as the canonical store). Under that fixed metric, the gap is empirical and falsifiable; under "16+ orders of magnitude" with implicit normalisation, the claim is closer to definitional.

## What's not falsifiable here (mathematical or definitional claims)

Several claims in the artifact are not refutable by any sweep, only by counterexample or definition. Listing them honestly is part of the Popperian discipline.

- **"The verifier-fidelity term collapses to float64 round-off through the Donzé-Maler evaluator's min/max depth."** This is a theorem about IEEE 754 ulp accumulation under min/max recursion, not an empirical claim. Falsifiable by a counterexample showing > 12 ulp accumulation at depth $\\le 12$, but not by any sweep over specs.
- **"The Goodhart decomposition $R\_{\\text{gold}} - R\_{\\text{proxy}} = (R\_{\\text{gold}} - R\_{\\text{spec}}) + (R\_{\\text{spec}} - R\_{\\text{verifier}})$ is auditable in stl-seed."** This is an algebraic identity; what is empirical is the *magnitude* of each term, not the decomposition itself.
- **The landscape theorem itself.** The theorem statement (regime I with PL alignment + directional vocabulary coverage, regime II with cliff, regime III with vocabulary-corner satisfiability) is mathematical. What is empirical is the *placement* of each task into a regime; the theorem is falsifiable only by a flaw in the proof, not by a sweep.
- **"Vocabulary-by-construction is a structural property, not a free win."** This is a definitional / philosophical claim about how to credit a method. It is not refutable by experiment.
- **"Three seeds is sized for falsification, not for population estimates."** This is a methodological choice, not a hypothesis.

The honest reading is that the empirical claims (1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7) are sharply falsifiable; the math claims (5 second-order, 8 first-order) are weakly falsifiable because the noise floor is partly definitional; and the "SERA-saturation extrapolates" claim (3) is the most load-bearing single experiment whose result we cannot yet predict from the existing data. Pre-registering its falsification rule in advance of the rotation compute is the intended next step.
